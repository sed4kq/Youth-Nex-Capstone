RESEARCH INTERESTS
My research examines how Black and Latinx youth draw upon their racial identity (i.e., the process
and meaning associated with the role of race in one’s life) and critical consciousness (i.e., one’s
awareness of social inequality and the tools, beliefs, and actions used to challenge such
inequality) development as motivators for their academic and civic engagement. My program of
research has three central foci: 1) integrating ethnic-racial identity and critical consciousness
factors as dual promoters of positive Black and Latinx adolescent and emerging adult development, 2)
examining how both ethnic-racial identity and critical consciousness promote academic achievement,
and 3) assessing the measurement complexity of ethnic-racial identity. My current work focuses on
how both ethnic-racial identity and critical consciousness processes are promotive in STEM contexts
both in the United States and the United Kingdom.
Keywords: ethnic racial identity, critical consciousness, sociopolitical development, psychometrics
